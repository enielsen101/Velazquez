Who Is Nydia Velazquez?

Ezra Nielsen | Oct. 8, 2024

Representative Nydia Velazquez (D-NY) was born in Puerto Rico and attended the University of Puerto Rico and New York University. In 1984 she served on the City Council, and in 1992 she became the first Puerto Rican woman elected to the U.S. House of Representatives. She represented New York's 12th Congressional District from 1993 to 2013. Since 2013 she has represented New York’s 7th Congressional District, which covers parts of Brooklyn and Queens. She is the Ranking Member of the House Small Business Committee.

Velazquez’s 16th term in Congress ends on Jan. 3, 2025. She is on the ballot in the general election on Nov. 5, 2024 and will be running against William Kregler (R/Conservative Party). But the Democratic and Working Families Party primaries were canceled because there were no competitors. These are the issues that have been central to Velazquez’s career and where she stands on them.

Healthcare

Velazquez opposed repeated efforts to repeal President Barack Obama’s Affordable Care Act. She introduced new health care centers to her district. Velazquez also supports insurance coverage for people with pre-existing conditions.

Housing

Velazquez has argued that mixed-use sites that become residential should be required to have 30-40% of their units be affordable housing. In 2023, she introduced the Landlord Accountability Act, which aimed to hold landlords accountable for discriminating against people with rental assistance vouchers and to fine property owners $100,000 every month they are found to “deliberately” leave an apartment empty.

Small Businesses

Velazquez is the Ranking Member of the House Small Business Committee. In 2023 she and other politicians, such as Senator Bob Mennedez (D-NJ) and Representative Warren Davidson (R-OH), introduced a bipartisan bill to protect insurance companies who work with cannabis sellers. The same year she and Menendez introduced a bill to protect small businesses from predatory lenders.

Puerto Rico

Velazquez is from Puerto Rico and was in part educated there. In December, 2023 she introduced a bipartisan bill to require FEMA to address recovery challenges in the U.S. territories. She also introduced the Puerto Rico Status Act, which was co-sponsored by Alexandria Ocasio Cortez. The bill allowed for Puerto Rico to choose its determination among three options. That bill passed the house and the senate with bipartisan support and President Biden signed it into law in 2022. In the summer of 2024 Velazquez called on the U.S. Department of Energy to track economic losses due to recurrent power outages on the island.
